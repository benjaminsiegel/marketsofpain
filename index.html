Markets of Pain

Tracing the Historical Trade in Opium

About the Author

This research explores the complex historical networks of the opium trade, examining the economic, social, and human dimensions of one of history's most controversial commodities. Through archival research and historical analysis, we trace the pathways of pain and profit that shaped empires and devastated communities.

The Historical Trade

The opium trade represents one of the most significant and troubling chapters in the history of global commerce. From the poppy fields of India to the factories of Patna, from the trading houses of London to the ports of China, this trade created vast fortunes while inflicting immeasurable suffering.

General Plan of the Opium Factory at Patna - A key site in the colonial opium trade

Through careful examination of historical documents, factory plans, and trade records, this project illuminates the infrastructure and mechanisms that sustained this global trade network and its lasting impact on societies across the world.
